## Overview

Use this checklist to avoid common UX writing issues that can hurt clarity, consistency, or global understanding.

## Content and clarity

- ❌ Don't use jargon or technical terms without explanation
  ✅ Use plain language that works for all users
- ❌ Don't write vague or generic messages
  ✅ Be specific about what happened, what's needed, or what's changing
- ❌ Don't overuse placeholder words like "thing," "this," or "stuff"
  ✅ Name the object, action, or result clearly
- ❌ Don't make assumptions about user knowledge
  ✅ Provide just enough context to orient new users without overwhelming returning ones
- ❌ Don't describe implementation instead of outcome
  ✅ Focus on what the user sees or does — not the backend logic

## Tone and voice

- ❌ Don't blame the user (e.g. "You forgot to...")
  ✅ Use neutral or shared language (e.g. "We couldn't find...")
- ❌ Don't use overly casual phrases (e.g. "Oops!" "No worries!")
  ✅ Keep the tone warm and professional
- ❌ Don't use humor, idioms, or sarcasm
  ✅ Be clear, respectful, and global-first in tone
- ❌ Don't publish AI-generated content without review
  ✅ Always edit for brand voice, clarity, and usefulness — especially if the AI references itself
- ❌ Don't use confirmshaming — framing the dismiss option as a negative or self-deprecating choice
  ✅ Use neutral dismiss language like "Cancel," "Not now," or "No thanks"
  - ❌ "No thanks, I don't want to improve my workflow"
  - ✅ "Not now"

## Style and formatting

- ❌ Don't capitalize words for emphasis
  ✅ Follow sentence case and capitalization rules
- ❌ Don't use multiple punctuation marks (e.g. "!" or "?!")
  ✅ Use one period or question mark as needed
- ❌ Don't cram multiple ideas into one sentence
  ✅ Keep messages scannable, short, and focused

## Terminology and consistency

- ❌ Don't invent new terms for existing concepts
  ✅ Reuse established terminology from the product glossary
- ❌ Don't mix naming conventions (e.g. "Views" vs "Saved Views")
  ✅ Match labels used in the UI exactly to avoid confusion

## Localization and accessibility

- ❌ Don't use metaphors, wordplay, or region-specific references
  ✅ Write literally and avoid cultural assumptions
- ❌ Don't concatenate strings for UI copy
  ✅ Keep full phrases intact for easy translation
- ❌ Don't rely on color or icon-only content without labels
  ✅ Add accessible text for clarity and screen readers
- ❌ Don't use directional language like "above," "below," "left," or "right" to reference UI elements
  ✅ Refer to the element by name or label instead
  - ❌ "Use the dropdown above to filter results"
  - ✅ "Use the **Filter** dropdown to filter results"
- ❌ Don't use Latin abbreviations (e.g., via, per, i.e., e.g.)
  ✅ Write them out in plain English (see localization guidelines)
- ❌ Don't use non-contracted phrasing where a contraction is natural
  ✅ "you'll" not "you will," "can't" not "cannot" (unless emphasis is needed)

## CTA and interaction design

- ❌ Don't use vague button labels like "OK" or "Submit"
  ✅ Use action-based labels that reflect the result (e.g. "Save changes")
- ❌ Don't stack multiple CTAs without hierarchy
  ✅ Prioritize the primary action and clearly separate secondary or cancel options
