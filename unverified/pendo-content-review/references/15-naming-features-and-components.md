## Overview

This file outlines how to name new product features, UI components, and capabilities across Pendo. Good names reflect what something is, how it works, and why it matters — without sounding like marketing.

Use this file when working with PMs, PMMs, or designers on naming experiences in the product.

## Naming principles

1. **Be literal and clear**
   - Choose names users understand without explanation
   - Avoid abstract, ambiguous, or metaphorical terms
2. **Communicate function**
   - A name should reflect what the feature *does*, not just what it's called
3. **Stay consistent**
   - Reuse familiar terms across surfaces and features
   - Align with existing taxonomy and mental models
4. **Show benefit when helpful**
   - Focus on value, especially for new features
   - Avoid empty or hype-driven names
5. **Prefer longevity**
   - Pick names that won't break with product evolution
   - Avoid technical terms that may become outdated
6. **Align with brand voice**
   - Simple, helpful, and clear — never clever for the sake of it
   - Friendly but not casual; confident but not salesy

## Good naming vs. bad naming

| Bad name | Why it's bad | Better name | Why it's better |
|---|---|---|---|
| Groups | Too generic; conflicts with known term | Teams | Describes functionality more clearly |
| Outcomes | Abstract; unclear what it does | Guide experiments | Describes both the method and context |
| Dashboard Quick Views | Verbose, unclear priority | Saved views | Short, familiar, actionable |
| Smarts | Vague and gimmicky | Recommendations | Clear and task-oriented |

## Tips for naming

Whether you're a writer, designer, PM, or engineer contributing to a naming decision:

- Use nouns that clearly represent the object, action, or concept
- Avoid cleverness or invented words unless they're part of branding
- Consider how the term would be understood by a new Pendo user
- Reflect the feature's purpose, not just its mechanics or UI location
- Mirror customer language when possible (e.g. terms from support tickets or research)
- Test for conflicts with existing UI or glossary terms

## Collaborating on naming

When you're involved in naming a feature or capability:
- Ask to see the functionality in context
- Clarify what the feature *does*, *affects*, and *solves*
- Identify familiar terms from other product areas
- Review the glossary for alignment or conflicts
- Follow the naming decision process below based on the type and level of the release

## Naming decision process

Use this process when something new needs a name — a feature, a nav label, or a capability.

**Essential rule: Post any naming update in #terms-in-pendo.**

| Level | What's being named | Who leads | Who's consulted | When to decide |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Products, modules, marketable capabilities | PMM | Content design, PMs, and execs | Early — before options are socialized |
| 2 | Features, nav elements | Content design | Product design + product manager | Before it gets coded |
| 3 | UI labels, microcopy | Content design | Product design | ASAP — check the glossary, decide, move on |

If a Level 3 term could set a precedent, treat it as Level 2. Not sure what level? Start at Level 2.

**How it works:**
1. Post in #terms-in-pendo with context.
2. Decision owner drives it.
3. Close the loop — post the decision and update the glossary.
