## Overview

Effective onboarding and in-app messages help users build confidence, understand value quickly, and form product habits. Use this guidance to write helpful, accessible, and globally friendly content for guides, walkthroughs, announcements, and welcome messages.

## Goals of onboarding

- Set clear expectations for what users can do or learn
- Explain what's happening and why it matters
- Offer reassurance and value, not hype or clutter
- Avoid over-guidance — keep each step purposeful

## General principles

- **Be user-focused**: It's about their task, not your product
- **Be literal and clear**: Say exactly what the guide is for
- **Be respectful of time**: Use only the steps you need
- **Be kind and calming**: Avoid urgent or salesy tone

## Structure of a good guide step

| Element | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Headline | Use sentence case, front-load the value or task |
| Body text | 1–2 sentences max. Say what's happening and why it matters |
| CTA buttons | Use action verbs ("Start tour," "Add segment"); no vague labels |
| Dismiss option | Always provide a non-primary way to exit or postpone |

## Use cases

| Guide type | Use for | Don't use for |
|---|---|---|
| Welcome message | Introductions, new features, setting expectations | Overloading with detail or promotions |
| Tooltip guides | Contextual tips, clarifications, micro-onboarding | Critical tasks or core flows |
| Walkthroughs | Multi-step flows, feature setup, admin onboarding | Low-friction features or one-step tasks |
| Announcement | New features, policies, upcoming changes | Passive promotions without user value |

## Tone guidance

| Situation | Tone to use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| First-time setup | Friendly, encouraging | "Start by adding your first Feature to get insights." |
| New feature discovery | Confident, helpful | "Here's a new way to compare usage over time." |
| Friction point | Calm, empathetic, supportive | "You'll need to connect metadata before using this view." |
| Optional tip | Reassuring, low-pressure | "You can customize this later in your settings." |

## Avoid

- ❌ Passive or generic copy: "Here's a tooltip with more info."
- ❌ Sales language: "Unlock your full potential!"
- ❌ Blame or pressure: "You haven't done this yet..."
- ❌ Too many steps — users abandon long walkthroughs

## Examples

✅ Good:
> **Welcome to your dashboard**
> This view helps you monitor key trends. Use filters to tailor the data.

✅ Good:
> **Start by tagging Features**
> Tag elements in your app to begin collecting analytics.

✅ Good:
> **Compare usage by segment**
> Use filters to uncover behavioral differences across key groups.

❌ Bad:
> **Unlock the power of analytics!**
> Our new feature is ready. Let's go!
