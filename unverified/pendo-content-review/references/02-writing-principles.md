## Core principles

These principles guide every piece of UX content we create:

1. **Be clear**
   - Prioritize comprehension. Avoid ambiguity.
   - Use literal, globally friendly phrasing.
2. **Be concise**
   - Don't say more than you need to. Users skim.
   - Favor short, meaningful sentences.
3. **Be useful**
   - Help users take action or understand context.
   - Always write for the task — not the product.
4. **Be consistent**
   - Follow capitalization, terminology, and formatting guidelines.
   - Align with approved glossary and patterns.
5. **Be kind**
   - Respect user emotions — especially during friction.
   - Avoid blame, negativity, or overly directive language.

## Patterns to reinforce

- Favor progressive disclosure: reveal only what's needed.
- Use active voice and plain language.
- Emphasize value or next steps, not just state changes.
- Write for scanning — use structure, bullets, and headings as needed.

## Voice alignment

Reinforce brand attributes:
- **Dependable**: Provide clarity and context, not fluff.
- **Passionate**: Support success without overselling.
- **Productive**: Help users get things done efficiently.
- **Honest**: Say only what's true — don't overpromise.

## Build a clear, empathetic narrative

- UX writing guides users through the product journey — think like a narrator.
- Your user is the hero; help them feel confident and supported.
- Deliver one idea at a time. Don't overload or distract.
- Support emotional states without being overly apologetic or cutesy.
- Think like a coach: Be informative, patient, and encouraging — not directive or robotic.

## Additional guidance for UI content

- Use second person ("you") — never refer to "users" or "customers."
- Avoid repeating the same word twice on the same screen — unless it reinforces clarity.

  ✅ Repeat intentionally:
  - "Add this Feature to a Product Area."
  - "These apps will default to Data User access only. You can update the role from the Applications page."

  ❌ Avoid when redundant:
  - "You can edit the app's settings from the app's settings panel."
  - "Create a dashboard from the dashboard creation window."

- Avoid abbreviations, initialisms, and acronyms unless the short form is better known or space-constrained (e.g. URL).
- Use sentence case throughout the UI, except for global navigation and boolean values.
- Avoid negative phrasing when a clearer positive alternative is available.
- Know who you're writing for, what the product does, and what the user needs to accomplish.
- Review or write content with full awareness of context, terminology, and potential use cases.
- Be proactive where possible. Anticipate user needs before confusion occurs.
